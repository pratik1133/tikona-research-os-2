Investment Thesis — Three Engines, One Re-rating Story

Core Thesis. Reliance is at a rare multi-segment inflection point. Consumer businesses now exceed 40% of revenues, Jio's H1 2026 IPO is expected to unlock $100B+ in value, and New Energy capex is transitioning from spend to earnings. Three reinforcing catalysts justify a structural re-rating and our ₹1,717 base-case target — 22% upside from CMP of ₹1,408.

Jio IPO — Digital Value Unlock: 515M+ subscribers and 50.9% wireless market share underpin a $100B+ valuation. IPO filing in H1 2026 is the single largest near-term catalyst to compress the conglomerate discount.

Retail — Scale Monetisation: 349M registered users and India's largest organised retail footprint provide a durable GMV growth engine. Omnichannel integration drives operating leverage and margin expansion through Mar-28E.

O2C — Resilient Cash Engine: World's largest integrated refinery at 1.4M bpd generates consistent free cash flow, funding digital and green investments while maintaining investment-grade credit (S&P A-).

Balance sheet credibility: S&P A- credit rating (two-notch upgrade) and Fortune Global 500 membership underpin Reliance's ability to fund the New Energy transition at scale.

Bear Case Scenarios — Stress-Tested Against Thesis

O2C Cyclicality Deepens: Global ethylene utilisation at ~80% and multi-year low chemical margins could suppress O2C EBITDA. Jio and Retail now contribute majority of group EBITDA, limiting downside to consolidated earnings.

New Energy Delays / Cost Overruns: Delivering acceptable returns on $80B+ post-COVID investments requires near-flawless execution. Phased commissioning of the Giga Complex and strategic partnerships reduce but do not eliminate this risk.

Telecom ARPU Pressure: Intensifying competition from Airtel (31.3% share) could slow ARPU expansion. Jio's 500M+ subscriber base and 5G rollout provide structural pricing power to absorb competitive pressure.

Macro / Demand Shock: A global slowdown could compress energy demand and retail consumption simultaneously. India's structural consumption growth and Reliance's domestic revenue mix (~85%) provide meaningful insulation.
