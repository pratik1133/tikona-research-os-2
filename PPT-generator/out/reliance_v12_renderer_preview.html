03Investment Thesis — Convergence of Catalysts in 2025–27

The Core Argument: A Re-Rating the Market Has Not Priced. Reliance's EPS compounded at just ~5% over FY22–25 as peak capex and O2C margin compression masked underlying growth. From FY25–27E, three simultaneous inflections — Jio ARPU hikes lifting digital EBITDA, retail operating leverage converting scale into margin, and new energy's first commercial revenues — drive EPS CAGR re-acceleration to ~15%. At 20.9x FY26E PE, the market is still pricing the old Reliance.

Three Pillars, One Convergence Window
Pillar 1 — Jio ARPU Expansion (Earnings Visibility)

Organic ARPU growth of 5–6% per annum via 5G adoption and home broadband penetration compounds directly into EBITDA with near-zero incremental capex. Digital services now carry the highest margin profile in the portfolio; each ₹10 ARPU lift adds ~₹6,500 Cr to annualised EBITDA. Jio IPO optionality is a valuation unlock addressed separately on page 10.

Pillar 2 — Retail Operating Leverage (Margin Inflection)

With 349M registered users and an omnichannel infrastructure already built, incremental revenue flows at structurally higher margins — private label mix expansion and JioMart digital integration are the key levers. Revenue CAGR of ~11.5% (FY25–28E) on a largely fixed cost base drives meaningful EBITDA margin expansion.

Pillar 3 — New Energy First Revenues (Multiple Expansion Trigger)

Solar PV manufacturing scaling to 10 GW and battery gigafactory commissioning in 2026 convert a pure-capex story into a revenue-generating green platform. The ₹75,000 Cr new energy investment is now entering its monetisation phase, warranting a re-rating toward clean-energy peer multiples rather than O&G.

Dimension to Watch — Capital Allocation Under Peak Capex (Falsifiable Risk)

The thesis breaks if consolidated capex remains above ₹150,000 Cr per annum beyond FY26, preventing OCF/EBITDA from recovering toward the 0.99x we model for FY27–28E. A sustained OCF/EBITDA below 0.65x for two consecutive halves would signal capex overrun and trigger thesis review.
